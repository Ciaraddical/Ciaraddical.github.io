What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

1- a lot of my identity revolves around being seen to be smart - I find struggling to learn something really challenging

2- I find helping others learn really rewarding - it increases my self-image in a constructive way

3- I get a far greater sense of satisfaction when I solve something if I’ve struggled with it - the struggle seems worth it for the endorphin hit

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Values - I feel that if we have a clear understanding of our values and motivations we can approach something - a project, or a problem, with more intent and self awareness. This aids in maintaining focus and encourages altruism

Empathy - one of the keys to successful cooperation, the ability to step into someone else’s shoes is also useful while learning - understanding the difference between how people learn also fosters self-compassion and a growth mindset.

Self-awareness - programming seems to almost invariably lead to self-awareness - we stare at code we imagine to be flawless for hours before someone else points out a mistake, and the realisation that we are viewing something with warped senses falls into place. The value of self awareness in learning is equally vital - the ability to recognise patterns in the way we respond to problems, stressors, and frustrations is the first step towards redirecting these responses in a more positive and constructive direction.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

I feel like core learning has filled gap in my knowledge that has been there for a while - ways to approach new knowledge, increase productivity, and deal with challenges - these were all things I had previously seen as somewhat hardwired.

I feel like without the core curriculum I would have struggled significantly more with the volume of information we’re expected to learn, as well as the experience of self directed remote learning.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

It’s brought to light that a lot of the excuses I’ve been leaning on are actually surmountable problems, and that ultimately, I’m responsible for my successes and failures. This is a somewhat uncomfortable truth, and I’m still learning how to balance the moments when I need to listen to my body and take a break, and when I need to push through discomfort and just do the work.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

As mentioned above, I don’t know if it would be possible to learn such a vast amount of information without also learning how to learn, how to handle emotions like frustration and self-doubt, and learning how to ask for help when needed.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

It doesn’t feel like a waste - although at the beginning of foundations I felt like the core curriculum wouldn’t teach me anything I didn’t already know, I’ve learned a lot in the last five weeks.

Ultimately, learning to program is a useful skill, but not as time-sensitive as the core content, I believe. Online resources will always be available (touch wood), but the core curriculum is vital to making learning to program possible, I think. It’s not a step you can really skip, if you want to progress and improve your capability to learn.
